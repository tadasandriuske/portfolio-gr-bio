About Me

PhD in Molecular and Cell Biology

Not everybody is lucky enough to be born perfectly healthy, and I happen to be one of the less fortunate. Regardless, the quality of my life is comparable to that of others, owing to the pharmaceutical innovations developed by brilliant scientists. This deep personal appreciation of the meaningful impact life sciences can have in the real world is what inspired me to pursue higher education degrees in biology. During my PhD studies, I realized that I prefer the applied aspects of science to those pertaining to purely curiosity-driven blue-sky research. For example, my favourite part of laboratory work was envisioning, designing, and creating complex strains of yeast which I could then use to answer questions about the mechanisms of DNA repair pathways more easily. Therefore, I am now seeking opportunities to continue my scientific career in industry.
